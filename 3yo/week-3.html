Week 3

Week 3 introduces answering simple questions in communication, ball kicking as the object control FMS focus, cause-and-effect in cognitive, joint attention in social-emotional, and handwashing as a self-help routine. At 3 years, kicking introduces technique cues more explicitly than at 2 years — children can follow and imitate verbal technique prompts.

Gross Motor Pod
Object Control: Kicking
FMS: Kicking
▾
Why This Matters

At 3 years, kicking is moving from an inconsistent punt to a more controlled kick with an approach step. The critical technique introduction this week is 'step next to the ball' — children who learn this foundation show significantly better kicking form by age 5. The 'use your laces not your toe' cue begins to differentiate quality of contact.

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Step next to the ball
Eyes on the ball
Use your laces not your toe
Swing the kicking leg through
Follow through after contact — don't stop at the ball
Activities
  • Kick stationary ball (EYLF 3): Large ball stationary on the ground. Approach and kick. Let the child calibrate their own approach distance.
  • Kick into goal (EYLF 3): Two cones as goalposts. Wide goal, close distance. 'Step next to it, then kick!'
  • Roll + kick game (EYLF 3): Adult rolls a slow ball toward the child who kicks it back. Adds tracking and timing to the kicking challenge.

VariationBalloons — much slower movement gives more time to prepare the approach step and makes success more likely.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Kick a ball together at the park or in the backyard. Say 'step next to it, then kick' out loud as a prompt every time. Count how many kicks land on target.

🫀 Body Awareness This Week

At snack: "Before you eat, check in — is your tummy really hungry, a little bit hungry, or not hungry yet?" Three options gives children language for gradations of hunger — not just hungry/not hungry.

Equipment
Large soft ballsGoal cones (2)Balloons (variation)

Communication Pod
Answering Questions — Where, What, Who
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

Answering questions is a crucial communicative milestone — it requires the child to hold the question in working memory, search for the answer, and produce an appropriate response. At 3, we extend beyond yes/no to 'where,' 'what,' and 'who' questions — all achievable targets. Most 3-year-olds can begin 'why' questions by end of this program.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — this week focus on Listen. When you ask 'where's the ball?' count silently to five before answering for them. Children often know but need the processing time.

Activities
  • Ask 'where's the ball?' (EYLF 5): Simple location questions. Wait 5 full seconds. Point as a cue only if still needed after that.
  • Yes/no in routines (EYLF 5): 'Do you want more?' 'Ready to go?' — authentic communication, not drilled.
  • Puppet asks questions (EYLF 5): Puppet asks 'what is this?' 'where did it go?' Children answer puppets more readily than adults.

VariationUse 'where' questions about objects that are genuinely hidden — links to the cognitive pod's object permanence work.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Describe and Narrate
🏠 Try This at Home

This week, ask one 'where' and one 'what' question before every snack. Wait 5 full seconds before giving a clue. Notice if your child starts answering faster by the end of the week.

Cognitive Pod
Cause and Effect
EF: WM (hold the 'if X then Y' model)
▾
Why This Matters

Harvard (2014): when a child deliberately repeats an action to produce a result, they are building working memory (holding the anticipated outcome) and inhibitory control (waiting to see the result before acting again). At 3, the key extension is simple prediction: 'what do you think will happen?' — building metacognition alongside the physical exploration.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Working Memory (hold the 'if X then Y' model) + Inhibitory Control (wait and observe before acting again)

Activities
  • Push button toy (EYLF 4): Pop-up or light-up toys. Let child discover the mechanism. Then ask: 'what do you think will happen if you press it again?'
  • Ball down ramp (EYLF 4): Board and books ramp. Roll balls of different weights. Which goes further? Let the child predict before releasing.
  • Deliberate drop and observe (EYLF 3): Drop different objects from a height. 'What happened? Will that one do the same thing?'

VariationWater pouring into different containers — compare levels, introduce early conservation ('is there more water now, or the same?').

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Follow Child's Lead
🏠 Try This at Home

Build a ramp at home — a folder against some books. Roll different objects: ball, block, crayon. Which goes furthest? Ask 'what do you think will happen?' before each roll.

Equipment
Push-button or pop-up toysBall ramp (board + books)Balls of varying weights/sizes

Social-Emotional Pod
Joint Attention — Following and Initiating
CASEL: Social Awareness
▾
Why This Matters

Joint attention — sharing focus on an object with another person — is one of the most predictive social-cognitive milestones for language and peer relationship outcomes. At 3, the target extends beyond following another's attention to initiating joint attention: the child points to something to share it with you, not just to request. This is a qualitatively different and more sophisticated social act.

Activities
  • Point and name (EYLF 5): Adult points to an object and names it. Child follows gaze/point. Then swap: child points, adult follows enthusiastically.
  • Shared book (EYLF 5): Read together. Follow the child's pointing to illustrations — don't redirect to the 'relevant' picture. What they point to is what matters.
  • Follow adult gaze (EYLF 4): Look at something deliberately without pointing — can the child follow your gaze to find what you're looking at?

VariationTorch game in a dimmed space — shine light on objects, child names them. Following a torch beam is highly motivating and builds both joint attention and visual tracking.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Social awareness: the child who can share their experience of the world — 'look at that!' — is building the social cognition that will support friendship, empathy, and learning.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Warm Attention — Follow the Child

Parents learn that following the child's pointing — rather than redirecting attention to the 'right' thing — is the most powerful joint attention intervention available. The child's attention is the curriculum.

🏠 Try This at Home

During one meal or bath this week, follow your child's pointing entirely. Whatever they point to, name it and add a detail. Let them lead what you talk about for 5 minutes.

Equipment
Shared picture booksTorch (optional)Interesting objects to point at

Self-Help Pod
Handwashing Routine — Full Sequence
ADL: Hygiene
▾
Why This Matters

At 3, the handwashing goal shifts from following an adult-led sequence to beginning to self-initiate. The song provides a duration cue (20 seconds) and a memory scaffold (what comes next in the sequence). By end of this program, the aim is for children to initiate handwashing before meals without prompting.

Activities
  • Pretend wipe practice (EYLF 3): Baby wipes for a quick accessible version — builds hand-rubbing motion in a low-stakes context.
  • Real handwashing — full sequence (EYLF 3): Wet → soap → rub (sing the song) → rinse → dry. Same words, same order, every time.
  • Dry on towel (EYLF 3): Drying is often skipped by 3-year-olds who consider themselves done after rinsing. Practise it explicitly.

VariationTeach children to check: 'are my hands clean? Do they smell like soap?' Builds the self-monitoring skill.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

At 3, parents often do a quick wipe rather than full handwashing because it's faster. The message: 30 real seconds of soap now creates a lifetime hygiene habit. A wipe creates a shortcut habit.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Initiative: the 3-year-old who initiates handwashing before meals — without being told — is demonstrating self-directed purposeful action in the world.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Support Then Release
🏠 Try This at Home

Handwashing before every meal and after every toilet visit this week — same song, same steps, every time. See if your child starts to go to the sink before you prompt them.

Equipment
Soap dispenser at child heightTowel at child heightStep stool if needed
