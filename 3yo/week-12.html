Week 12

Week 12 is consolidation and celebration. The focus across all domains is building on the skills from weeks 1–11, acknowledging how far children and parents have come, and setting families up for continued practice beyond the program. The final session is about community — naming peers, celebrating shared experience, and honouring the parents' own learning.

Gross Motor Pod
Fine Motor: Pre-Writing Marks — Lines, Circles, and Crosses
FMS: Pre-Writing Shapes
▾
Why This Matters

By end of the 3-year-old program, the pre-writing target is intentional production of horizontal lines, vertical lines, circular marks, and beginning cross formation. These four marks contain the building blocks of all English letters and numbers. Drawing a face — circle, two eyes, a mouth — is a developmentally significant milestone that integrates fine motor skill, symbolic representation, and spatial organisation.

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Hold with tripod grip — three fingers
Horizontal line — pull across the page
Vertical line — push down
Circular scribble — keep going around and around
Cross — one line down, one line across
Activities
  • Lines on demand (EYLF 4): 'Can you draw me a line that goes across? Now one that goes down?' Name and demonstrate each.
  • Trace in sand (EYLF 4): Trace over adult-drawn lines in a sand tray — builds motor memory.
  • Large brushes on paper (EYLF 4): Broad deliberate strokes. Process, not product.

VariationDraw a face together — 'let's draw a circle for the head. Now two eyes. Now a mouth.' This is the most meaningful fine motor milestone of the 3-year-old program.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate Growth

Parents name what they can see in their child's fine motor development now compared to Week 1.

🏠 Try This at Home

Draw lines and circles together on big paper. Ask: 'can you draw a face?' A circle with two dots and a line is genuinely impressive. Praise the effort and the specific achievement.

Large paperPaint and brushesSand trayCrayons

Communication Pod
Vocabulary Celebration — 50+ Words and Growing Sentences
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

By 36 months, children with typical development have 200–300 words and are producing 3–4 word sentences. Many 3-year-olds entering Play Pods will not be at this level. This final week celebrates growth from each child's own starting point — not comparison to a milestone. The measure is progress from Week 1.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — final week: reflect on your own OWL practice. Which was easiest for you — Observe, Wait, or Listen? Which was hardest? Share with the group.

Activities
  • Vocabulary review (EYLF 5): Picture cards from all 12 weeks. How many can this child now name that they couldn't in Week 1?
  • Scavenger hunt (EYLF 2): Outdoor — how many things can we name in 5 minutes? Count as a group.
  • Pretend shop (EYLF 5): Items on a shelf, money, bag. Vocabulary and sentence structure in a social context.

VariationMake a personal 'word book' for each child — photographs of things they can now name. Send home with family.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate Growth — Not Milestones

Parents identify and name the communication growth from Week 1 to Week 12 for their own child. This is consolidation of the parent's own learning.

🏠 Try This at Home

Make a word book at home — photographs of 10 things your child can name. Look at it together each day. Add to it every week. The program ends — the language growth continues.

Equipment
Picture cards from weeks 1–12Camera/phone for photosWord book template

Cognitive Pod
Routine Mastery — Consolidation of EF Skills
EF: WM (multi-step routine)
▾
Why This Matters

The ability to complete a familiar routine — pack up, wash hands, come to the table — without individual prompting is the summative EF achievement of the 3-year-old program. Harvard (2014): a child who completes this sequence using only the group song as a cue is demonstrating working memory, inhibitory control, and cognitive flexibility integrated in a real-world context. This is what school readiness looks like.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Working Memory (multi-step routine) + Inhibitory Control (complete each step) + Cognitive Flexibility (routine in new setting)

Activities
  • Full pack-away without individual prompts (EYLF 3): Use only the pack-away song. Wait for the routine to complete without naming individual children.
  • Independent handwashing (EYLF 3): Complete the full sequence without reminders. Who initiates unprompted?
  • Snack transition — song only (EYLF 5): Move to the snack area using only the transition song as a cue. No verbal direction.

VariationTime the routine — 'let's see if we can do it in the same time as last week.' Builds self-monitoring and group coordination.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate Routine Mastery

Parents recognise how much EF their child is demonstrating in familiar routines — and commit to maintaining those routines at home after the program ends.

🏠 Try This at Home

Run one familiar home routine without verbal prompts — just the song or a signal. Notice how much your child can do independently now compared to Week 1. That's 12 weeks of practice.

Equipment
Pack-away songHandwashing stationSnack table setup

Social-Emotional Pod
Peer Recognition and Community Farewell
CASEL: Social Awareness
▾
Why This Matters

The final social-emotional session marks an ending. Endings matter developmentally — a well-managed ending teaches children that goodbyes are safe and that community persists beyond a single setting. Naming all their peers, identifying them in photos, and performing a shared farewell ritual consolidates the social identity work of 12 weeks.

Activities
  • Name all group peers (EYLF 1): 'Who's here today?' Can each child name all the others?
  • Group photo identification (EYLF 1): Photos of the group — 'who is this? do you know their name?'
  • Group farewell ritual (EYLF 1): Same farewell every time — song, wave, handshake. Consistent and warm.

VariationGroup photo card for each child to take home — 'my Play Pods friends.'

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Social identity: the 3-year-old who can name their peers and participate in a shared farewell understands themselves as a member of a community beyond the family. This is one of the most significant social achievements of early childhood.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate the Parent's Journey

The facilitator names and celebrates what parents have learned and practised over 12 weeks. The final message: 'you are now your child's best developmental partner — you know what to do, and you've proved it over 12 weeks.'

🏠 Try This at Home

Talk about the Play Pods friends at home — look at the group photo, name them. Ask about them. Community doesn't end when the program does.

Equipment
Group photo cards for each childFarewell songCertificate or memento for each family

Self-Help Pod
Carrying, Helping, and Celebrating Independence
ADL: All ADL categories
▾
Why This Matters

The final self-help session consolidates the IADL theme — social contribution through purposeful action. The child who can carry, deliver, and cooperate in pack-away at Week 12 has developed the integrated self-help independence that is the goal of the program. The graduation helper role gives each child a moment of special recognition.

Activities
  • Deliver cups to peers (EYLF 3): Child delivers cups to each peer at snack. Named recipients, real social outcome.
  • Carry own snack plate (EYLF 3): From preparation area to seat — fully independently.
  • Full pack-away (EYLF 3): Complete participation in group pack-away. No individual prompting.

VariationGive each child a special 'graduation helper job' for the final session — a role that acknowledges their specific growth.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Final session: parents may want to do everything for their child to make it special. Reframe: 'the most special thing you can do in this final session is step back and watch what they can now do independently. That is the achievement.'

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Industry consolidated: the child who completes Week 12 has been building the narrative 'I am capable and I contribute' for 12 weeks. That is the Eriksonian industry achievement of this program. It does not end here — it continues in every household routine, every meal, every dressing opportunity.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate and Release

Parents witness and name what their child can now do independently that they couldn't do 12 weeks ago. This is the summary of the program.

🏠 Try This at Home

Give your child a regular household helper role and keep it after the program ends. The program ends — the routine continues. They are capable. They know it. You know it.
