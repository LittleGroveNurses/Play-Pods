Week 11

Week 11 focuses on sentence expansion toward 4–5 word phrases in communication, hopping as the new locomotion FMS (a significant 3YO milestone), categorisation with three-way grouping in cognitive, cooperative play in social-emotional, and contextual dressing in self-help.

Gross Motor Pod
Locomotion: Hopping
FMS: Hopping
▾
Why This Matters

Hopping on one foot is a significant locomotor milestone that typically emerges at 3–4 years. It requires single-leg balance, rhythmic timing, and cross-lateral arm-leg coordination. At 3, 2–3 consecutive hops on the preferred foot is an appropriate target. Munch & Move (2018): 'stand on one leg and bend the opposite leg — spring off your foot — use arm and leg swing to help.'

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Stand on one leg — bend the opposite leg up
Spring off your standing foot
Use arm swing and leg swing to help you hop
Bend arms at elbows
Land on the same foot you took off from
Activities
  • Hop on the spot — preferred foot (EYLF 3): Start with balance on one leg, then add the spring. 2–3 consecutive hops is the Week 11 target.
  • Hopscotch hopping (EYLF 3): Hop from square to square on the preferred foot. Adult can hop alongside.
  • 'What's the Time Mr Fox?' — hopping (EYLF 3): Move forward by hopping. Builds hopping distance and control.

VariationHoop Count — children hop around the space to music, stop and group themselves in hoops when the music stops. Name the count.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Hopscotch at home — chalk or tape squares. Hop from square to square on one foot. Start on the preferred foot. Count how many hops in a row without putting the other foot down.

🫀 Body Awareness This Week

At snack: "Before you take more, check — does your tummy still want it, or are you eating because it's there?" This is an advanced sDOR concept for 3-year-olds — distinguishing appetite from habit eating.

Communication Pod
Expanding Sentences — 4–5 Word Utterances
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

By Week 11, children who have been consistently receiving the expansion technique for 10 weeks should be showing sentence growth. The target at 3 is 4–5 word utterances: 'I want the big red ball.' These combine the vocabulary built in weeks 1–5 with the grammatical structures built in weeks 6–8. Story recall is the highest level language task in this program — it requires narrative organisation, sequencing, and vocabulary deployment simultaneously.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — Observe this week whether your child is adding words without prompting. Celebrate every new element in a sentence — even if it's not in the right order.

Activities
  • Add descriptors (EYLF 5): Model 4–5 word sentences in play: 'I see a big red truck!' Wait for the child to try.
  • Role-play phone (EYLF 5): Toy phone conversation. Two-way exchange structure. Practise both asking and answering.
  • Story recall (EYLF 5): After a familiar book: 'what happened at the beginning? What happened next? What happened at the end?'

VariationPhotograph book of the program — look at photos from earlier sessions and tell what happened. Personal narrative with real events.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Describe and Narrate
🏠 Try This at Home

After any event this week — a trip out, a bath, dinner — ask: 'tell me what happened.' Accept any attempt and expand it back. 'You said big splash — yes, you made a HUGE splash in the water!'

Cognitive Pod
Categorisation — Three-Way Groups with Reasoning
EF: CF (three-way rule
▾
Why This Matters

Three-way categorisation with explanatory reasoning ('they all live on a farm' / 'they all travel in water') is a significant cognitive achievement at 3. Harvard (2014): children who can flexibly switch between sorting rules at this age show markedly stronger school readiness outcomes. This week targets the peak of the CF window identified by Blakey et al. (2015).

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Cognitive Flexibility (three-way rule + rule switch) + Working Memory (hold all three categories)

Activities
  • Farm/zoo/pet animal sort (EYLF 5): Three clear categories. Name the category rule before beginning. Ask why after each placement.
  • Road/sky/water transport (EYLF 4): Three-way sort. 'Why does the boat go here?' Building explanatory reasoning.
  • 'Which doesn't belong — and why?' (EYLF 5): Present groups of 3 with one odd one out. Name the reason.

VariationSwitch rule mid-task: after farm/zoo/pet sort — 'now let's sort them by colour instead.' The rule switch is the CF challenge.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Follow Child's Lead
🏠 Try This at Home

Sort the toy box with a rule, then switch: 'first animals vs vehicles, now let's sort by colour.' Notice if your child can switch the rule. If they can't yet, just do both separately.

Equipment
Mixed animal sets (farm, zoo, pets)Transport toys (road, air, water)Sorting containers

Social-Emotional Pod
Cooperative Play — Shared Goals
CASEL: Relationship Skills
▾
Why This Matters

Cooperative play — children working together toward a shared goal — is the most advanced social play milestone in the 3-year-old program. CASEL relationship skills at this level include communicating within a group, adjusting behaviour based on others' responses, and negotiating. The parachute game is a perfect cooperative activity because every single child must contribute for it to work.

Activities
  • Group pack-away (EYLF 2): 'Everyone put one toy in the box.' Simple shared goal. Use the pack-away song.
  • Circle clapping (EYLF 1): Everyone claps together for a count, then pauses together. Shared rhythm requires social coordination.
  • Parachute game (EYLF 2): Every child holds the parachute edge. Bounce a ball on it together. Requires genuine group coordination.

VariationBall roll around the parachute — roll the ball around the edge without letting it fall off. Requires watching others and timing your own movements.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Industry: the 3-year-old who contributes to a group goal — even in the smallest way — is experiencing the satisfaction of social usefulness. This is a profound developmental achievement.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Narrate the Cooperation

Parents learn to name and celebrate cooperative moments explicitly: 'did you see that? You and Maya worked together to keep the ball up — that was teamwork!'

🏠 Try This at Home

Cooperative challenge at home: 'let's move all the cushions to the bedroom together.' Count how many trips. Celebrate the completion: 'we did it — together!'

Equipment

Self-Help Pod
Contextual Dressing — Hat, Jacket, Weather Awareness
ADL: Dressing
▾
Why This Matters

Contextual dressing introduces the idea that dressing decisions are linked to environment and weather. This builds cognitive flexibility alongside the physical skill. At 3, the 'hat outside, no hat inside' rule is achievable. The jacket floor flip — laying the jacket on the floor, inserting arms, flipping over the head — is a technique many 3-year-olds can manage and find deeply satisfying.

Activities
  • Remove hat indoors (EYLF 3): Consistent prompt every time entering indoors. After 3 weeks of this, children often begin to self-initiate.
  • Put on hat outdoors (EYLF 3): Child manages hat before jackets. Velcro straps or elasticated hats are easiest.
  • Jacket floor flip (EYLF 3): Lay jacket on floor with hood away from child. Child puts arms in neck opening, flips it over their head. A genuine skill that 3-year-olds find satisfying.

VariationWeather talk: 'it's cold today — what do we need to wear?' Builds the weather–dressing rule-connection.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Parents carry children's hats and jackets rather than waiting for the child to manage them. Reframe: an extra minute at the door now builds an independent habit for school.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Initiative through rule-based action: the 3-year-old who removes their hat when coming indoors — without being told — is self-regulating based on an internalised rule. This is initiative directed at the self.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Support Then Release
🏠 Try This at Home

Every indoor-outdoor transition: wait for your child to manage their own hat. Teach the jacket floor flip at a non-urgent time first. Same words every time: 'hats off inside, hats on outside.'

Child's own hat and jacketLow hook at child heightMirror for checking
