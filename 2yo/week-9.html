Gross Motor Pod
Fine Motor: Utensil Use — Fork and Scoop
FMS: Tool Use (precision grip)
▾
Why This Matters

Utensil use requires precision grip, wrist supination, and hand-eye coordination. Playdough as a medium removes the consequence of dropping food and allows repetitive practice.

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Hold fork with three fingers
Point tines down toward food
Pierce or scoop — don't stab
Elbow up slightly on the way to the mouth
Eyes on the food
Activities
  • Fork snack (EYLF 3): Soft fruit on a plate. Child uses fork independently.
  • Pretend kitchen utensils (EYLF 4): Pretend cooking with real safe utensils.
  • Playdough scooping (EYLF 4): Small spoon to transfer playdough between containers.

VariationTraining chopsticks for older 2-year-olds — high grip challenge.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Playdough transfer at home — two containers, one small spoon. Count the scoops.

🫀 Body Awareness This Week

At snack, notice who finishes and who doesn't. Neither is better — both are their body working correctly. Coach parents: "Your job was the what, when, and where. Their job was the whether and how much."

Equipment
Soft fruit for fork practicePretend kitchen utensilsPlaydough and small spoons

Communication Pod
Early Storytelling — Sequence Three Pictures
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

Sequencing three pictures is the earliest form of narrative construction — beginning, middle, end. This skill is directly predictive of reading comprehension at school entry.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — Observe: when your child tells you something, notice the sequence — do they have a beginning? Follow their narrative lead before adding your own words.

Activities
  • Sequence 3 pictures (EYLF 5): Three pictures of a familiar event. Put them in order together.
  • Child describes play (EYLF 4): 'Tell me what happened with the blocks.' Accept any attempt.
  • Puppet show story (EYLF 5): Simple story with clear beginning, middle, end.

VariationDraw a simple 3-picture sequence together of something that just happened.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Describe and Narrate
🏠 Try This at Home

Make a 3-picture story of your morning routine. Put the pictures in order together: 'first... then... then...'

📚 Book of the Week — Hanen Book Nook

The Very Busy Spider — Eric Carle

Clear beginning-middle-end narrative — matching the early storytelling theme. The repeating sequence makes it easy for children to retell: 'first the horse came, then the cow...' Encourage retelling with the book closed.

Cognitive Pod
Shape Awareness
EF: WM (hold shape template in mind)
▾
Why This Matters

Shape recognition is the first step toward spatial reasoning and later literacy. The outdoor shape hunt extends the skill to real-world contexts — cognitive flexibility at its most natural.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Working Memory (hold shape template in mind) + Cognitive Flexibility (find shapes in new contexts)

Activities
  • Sort shapes (EYLF 4): Circle, square, triangle. One shape at a time.
  • Shape puzzle (EYLF 4): Model rotation of piece before handing back.
  • Outdoor shape hunt (EYLF 2): 'Find me a circle outside.' Windows, wheels, plates.

VariationDraw shapes in sand — adds kinesthetic memory.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Follow Child's Lead
🏠 Try This at Home

Go on a shape hunt in your home — find 3 circles, 3 squares, 3 rectangles. Photograph them.

Equipment
Shape sorting boardShape puzzlesChalk for outdoor shapes

Social-Emotional Pod
Responding to Name — Group Recognition
CASEL: Social Awareness + Relationship Skills
▾
Why This Matters

In a group context, hearing your name called — and responding — is the first experience of belonging to a community beyond the family.

Activities
  • Call name in group song (EYLF 5): 'Hello [name], how are you?'
  • Hide & seek call (EYLF 5): 'Where's [name]?' Child calls back or comes out.
  • Clap name syllables (EYLF 5): Clap the syllables of each child's name.

VariationMake name cards with each child's photo.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Social identity formation begins with reliable recognition that 'that sound means me.'

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Warm Attention

Parents learn that group belonging is a developmental need — naming and being named in a group is powerful.

🏠 Try This at Home

Use your child's name in songs at home: 'Old MacDonald had a [name]... E-I-E-I-O.' They will love it.

Equipment
Name song (adapted)Photo name cards

Self-Help Pod
Helping — Carry and Deliver
ADL: IADL
▾
Why This Matters

Helper tasks with real consequences build intrinsic motivation. This is qualitatively different from compliance.

Activities
  • Carry toy to shelf (EYLF 3): One toy from floor to shelf. Child chooses which.
  • Deliver block (EYLF 3): 'Can you take this block to [peer's name]?' Social + self-help.
  • Carry snack cup (EYLF 3): Child carries own cup from preparation area to seat.

VariationGive the child a small tray — makes the role feel official.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Parents give children 'pretend' helper tasks while doing the real task themselves. Only real tasks with real consequences build genuine industry.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Industry: the child who delivers something to a person experiences themselves as useful.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Support Then Release
🏠 Try This at Home

Give your child one real delivery job each day — carry the napkins, bring a toy to a sibling, take their cup to the sink.

Equipment
Small tray for carryingDesignated shelf at child height
