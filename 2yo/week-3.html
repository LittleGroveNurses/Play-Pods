Week 3

Week 3 introduces answering simple questions in communication, ball kicking as the gross motor FMS focus, cause-and-effect in cognitive, joint attention in social-emotional, and handwashing as a self-help routine.

Gross Motor Pod
Object Control: Kicking
FMS: Kicking
▾
Why This Matters

Kicking is the first object control FMS where the child's body and a projectile interact. At 2 years, children are typically making contact with a stationary ball. The key cue this week: 'step next to the ball' — the most important foundation for later kicking.

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Step next to the ball
Eyes on the ball
Use your laces not your toe
Swing the kicking leg through
Follow through after contact
Activities
  • Kick stationary ball (EYLF 3): Large ball stationary. Child approaches and kicks.
  • Kick into goal (EYLF 3): Two cones as goalposts. Wide goal, close distance. Celebrate any ball movement.
  • Roll + kick game (EYLF 3): Adult rolls a slow ball toward child who kicks it back.

VariationBalloons — slower movement builds confidence.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Kick a ball together in the backyard. Try 'step next to it, then kick' — say it out loud as a prompt.

Equipment
Large soft ballsGoal cones (2)Balloons

Communication Pod
Answering Simple Questions
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

Answering 'where' and 'yes/no' questions requires holding the question in working memory, searching for the answer, and producing a response. Most adults ask and immediately answer, removing the learning opportunity.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — this week focus on Listen. When you ask 'where's the ball?' wait the full 5 seconds before answering for them.

Activities
  • Ask 'where's ball?' (EYLF 5): Ask simple location questions and wait. Point as a cue only after 5 seconds.
  • Yes/no in routines (EYLF 5): Use yes/no questions at snack, dressing.
  • Puppet asks questions (EYLF 5): Children answer puppets more readily than adults.

VariationUse 'where' questions with hidden objects from the cognitive pod.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Describe and Narrate
🏠 Try This at Home

Ask one 'where' question before every snack. Wait 5 full seconds before giving a clue.

Equipment
PuppetFamiliar objects to hide

Cognitive Pod
Cause and Effect
EF: WM (hold 'if I do X, Y happens')
▾
Why This Matters

Harvard (2014): when a child deliberately repeats an action to produce a result, they are building working memory — holding the anticipated outcome — and inhibitory control — waiting to see the result before acting again.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Working Memory (hold 'if I do X, Y happens') + Inhibitory Control (wait and observe before repeating)

Activities
  • Push button toy (EYLF 4): Pop-up or light-up toys. Let the child discover the mechanism.
  • Ball down ramp (EYLF 4): Build a ramp from a board and books. Roll balls of different weights.
  • Drop spoon from high chair (EYLF 3): Intentional exploration, not misbehaviour. Name what happens.

VariationWater pouring into different containers — introduces early conservation concepts.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Follow Child's Lead
🏠 Try This at Home

Build a simple ramp at home — a folder against some books. Roll different balls. Which goes furthest?

Equipment
Pop-up or button toysBall ramp (board + books)Balls of different sizes

Social-Emotional Pod
Joint Attention
CASEL: Social Awareness
▾
Why This Matters

Joint attention — sharing focus on an object or event with another person — is one of the earliest and most critical social-cognitive milestones. Children who develop robust joint attention by 18 months show stronger language and social outcomes at school entry.

Activities
  • Point to toys (EYLF 5): Adult points to an object and names it — child looks. Then swap.
  • Shared book (EYLF 5): Read together with child in your lap. Follow the child's pointing.
  • Follow gaze (EYLF 4): Look at something without pointing — can the child follow your gaze?

VariationUse a torch in a darkened corner — shine it at objects and name them.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Social awareness begins with the recognition that other people have their own perspective and attention.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Warm Attention

Parents learn that following the child's pointing and gaze — rather than directing attention — is the most powerful joint attention intervention available.

🏠 Try This at Home

During one meal or bath, follow your child's pointing. Whatever they point to, name it and add a detail. Let them lead what you talk about.

Equipment
Shared picture booksTorch (optional)

Self-Help Pod
Handwashing Routine
ADL: Hygiene
▾
Why This Matters

Handwashing builds procedural memory. By making the routine consistent and paired with a song, the sequence becomes self-initiating within weeks.

Activities
  • Pretend wipes (EYLF 3): Baby wipes for an accessible version — builds the hand-rubbing motion.
  • Real handwashing with soap (EYLF 3): Full sequence: wet → soap → rub → rinse → dry.
  • Dry on towel (EYLF 3): Drying is often skipped — practise it explicitly.

VariationUse a handwashing song (20 seconds) to build in the right duration.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Parents often do a quick wipe rather than full handwashing to save time. The message: 30 real seconds of soap creates the habit. A wipe creates a shortcut.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Routine mastery: a child who can complete the handwashing sequence without prompting is experiencing genuine competence.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Support Then Release
🏠 Try This at Home

Handwashing before every meal this week — same song, same steps, every time. By Friday you should see your child starting to initiate it.

Equipment
Soap dispenser at child heightTowel at child heightStep stool if needed
