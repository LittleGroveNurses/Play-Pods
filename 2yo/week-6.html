Gross Motor Pod
Stability: Balance
FMS: Static and Dynamic Balance
▾
Why This Matters

Balance is the stability FMS underpinning all locomotion and object control skills. Diamond (2000): balance activities require focused attention and body monitoring — directly building EF alongside the physical skill.

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Eyes fixed on a point ahead
Arms out for balance
Soft knees — not locked
One foot at a time on the beam
Freeze like a statue
Activities
  • Walk low beam (EYLF 3): Low balance beam or line of tape. Arms out, eyes ahead.
  • Cushion balance (EYLF 3): Stand on a wobble cushion or pillow.
  • Freeze to music (EYLF 3): Hold a frozen pose when music stops — builds static balance and inhibitory control.

VariationCarry a beanbag on head while walking the beam.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Walk along a line of tape on the floor — heel to toe. See how far you can go without stepping off.

Equipment
Low balance beam or tape lineWobble cushionBeanbagMusic

Communication Pod
Using Plurals
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

Plurals emerge naturally from consistent modelling in context. Overusing singular forms with groups of objects delays plural acquisition.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — Observe: watch whether your child is noticing when there are more than one of something. They may be ready for plurals before they can produce them.

Activities
  • Show 2 toys 'cars' (EYLF 5): Hold up two identical toys: 'cars! Two cars!'
  • Farm play animals (EYLF 5): 'The cows! Three cows in the field.'
  • Story plural objects (EYLF 5): Choose books with plural nouns.

VariationDeliberately misuse plurals and see if the child corrects.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Describe and Narrate
🏠 Try This at Home

Point out plurals as you go through the day: 'shoes! two shoes. cups! three cups.'

Equipment
Matching toy pairsFarm set with multiple animalsBooks with plural nouns

Cognitive Pod
Memory — Hide and Pattern Repetition
EF: WM (remember location and sequence)
▾
Why This Matters

Harvard (2014): hiding games exercise working memory as the child tracks the object's location. Clapping patterns add sequential working memory challenge.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Working Memory (remember location and sequence)

Activities
  • Hide toy same spot (EYLF 4): Hide in same location 3 times, then move it.
  • Clap pattern repeat (EYLF 5): Two-clap pattern — child copies. Add a third clap.
  • Repeat routine songs (EYLF 5): Songs with repeated verses build sequential working memory.

VariationSpinning turntable to move hidden object.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Follow Child's Lead
🏠 Try This at Home

Play 'clap copy' at the table — you clap a pattern, they copy. Start with one clap. Build up.

Equipment
Small toys for hidingDrum for clapping patterns

Social-Emotional Pod
Stranger Awareness and New Adults
CASEL: Self-Management
▾
Why This Matters

Wariness of unfamiliar adults is developmentally appropriate — it reflects secure attachment. The goal is to give children emotional language and safe base to manage it, not to eliminate the wariness.

Activities
  • Meet new adult (EYLF 1): Introduce a new face to the group. Name them, let children observe from the caregiver's arms.
  • Familiar toy present (EYLF 1): Child's comfort object is visible throughout.
  • Group song with new adult (EYLF 5): New adult joins the group song — indirect exposure.

VariationHave the new adult bring a special object to share.

🎯 Facilitator Coaching Cues

When a parent pushes a shy child toward a new adult: "Let's give him a minute to watch from your arms — he's working up to it."

When a child refuses to engage: "She's showing us healthy attachment behaviour — she feels safe enough with you to say no to someone unfamiliar."

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Autonomy is built on security — a child who knows their parent will be there can take gradual social risks.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Safe Base — Stay Close

Parents understand that their calm, available presence is the scaffold for stranger regulation.

🏠 Try This at Home

When your child meets a new person this week, stay close, name the person, and let your child approach in their own time.

Equipment
Comfort objects accessible

Brushing Teeth
ADL: Hygiene
▾
Why This Matters

Making teeth brushing predictable, musical, and identity-building reduces resistance more effectively than instruction.

Activities
  • Pretend brush song (EYLF 3): Child brushes a doll's teeth while adult sings brushing song.
  • Real teeth brushing (EYLF 3): Child has a go first — adult finishes. Never skip the child's attempt.
  • Mirror practice (EYLF 3): Watch in mirror while brushing.

VariationLet the child choose their toothbrush from two options.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Parents often do the full brushing themselves to ensure it's done properly. An imperfect child-led brush followed by a parent finish is better than parent-only for building the routine.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Routine mastery: the child who participates in teeth brushing is building the sense of being responsible for their own body.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Support Then Release
🏠 Try This at Home

Let your child brush first every night — even for 10 seconds — before you take a turn. Same song every time.

Equipment
Child's toothbrushMirrorDoll for practice
