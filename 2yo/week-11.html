Gross Motor Pod
Locomotion: Jumping Off a Step
FMS: Two-Footed Jump from Height
▾
Why This Matters

Jumping from a height requires bilateral coordination, timing, and trust. The arm swing + knee bend sequence is critical for safe landing. Munch & Move (2018): 'swing your arms back and bend your knees, reach for the stars, bend knees to land.'

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Swing arms back and bend your knees before jumping
Jump with both feet together
Reach for the stars on take-off
Bend knees to land — soft landing
Look where you're landing
Activities
  • Jump to adult arms (EYLF 3): Adult stands at bottom of small step with arms open.
  • Jump to mat (EYLF 3): Land on a mat from a low step.
  • Imitation jumps (EYLF 3): Adult demonstrates various jump styles — child copies.

VariationLily pad jumping — hoops on the floor to jump between.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Jump off the bottom step at home with both feet together. Try to land with soft knees each time.

🫀 Body Awareness This Week

After jumping, try: "Did that feel big in your body? Did your legs feel strong or wobbly?" Teaching children to notice physical sensations after exertion builds the vocabulary for all internal body states.

Equipment
Low step (15–20cm)Landing matHoops for lily pads

Communication Pod
Expanding Sentences
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

By Week 11, children have been receiving the expansion technique for 10 weeks. This week consolidates sentence expansion and begins narrative recall.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — Observe this week whether your child is starting to add words without prompting. Celebrate every new word they add.

Activities
  • Add words ('big ball') (EYLF 5): Model 3–4 word sentences.
  • Role-play phone call (EYLF 5): Two-way conversation structure.
  • Story recall (EYLF 5): After reading: 'what happened to the bear? what did he do?'

VariationMake a short 'play video' and watch it back together, narrating what happened.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Describe and Narrate
🏠 Try This at Home

After any event: 'what did we do? tell me about it.' Accept any attempt and expand it.

📚 Book of the Week — Hanen Book Nook

Dinosaur Roar! — Paul & Henrietta Stickland

Four-to-five word descriptive phrases throughout — matching the sentence expansion theme. Opposites (big/small, fast/slow) build sentence length naturally. Pause at opposites and wait for your child to supply the pair.

Cognitive Pod
Categorisation — Animal Groups
EF: CF (switch between category types)
▾
Why This Matters

Harvard (2014): children switching between category rules are at the peak EF challenge for this age group.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Cognitive Flexibility (switch between category types) + Working Memory (hold rule while sorting)

Activities
  • Group animals (EYLF 5): Farm vs zoo vs pet. Name the sorting rule before beginning.
  • Farm pretend (EYLF 5): All farm animals in the paddock, all wild animals in the jungle.
  • Transport toys group (EYLF 4): Road vs sky vs water. Three-way sort is harder than two-way.

VariationChallenge: 'which one doesn't belong?' and explain why.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Follow Child's Lead
🏠 Try This at Home

Sort the toy box together — soft toys, vehicles, figures. Try changing the rule: 'now let's sort by colour.' The rule change is the challenge.

Equipment
Mixed animal setsTransport toysSorting trays

Social-Emotional Pod
Cooperative Play — Pack-Away and Parachute
CASEL: Relationship Skills
▾
Why This Matters

Cooperative play — working toward a shared goal — is the most advanced social play milestone in the 2-year-old program. The parachute game is one of the most effective cooperative experiences at this age because it requires every child to contribute for it to work.

Activities
  • Pack away toys (EYLF 2): Group pack-away — 'everyone put one toy in the box.'
  • Join clapping (EYLF 1): Circle clapping — everyone claps together, then pauses together.
  • Parachute game (EYLF 2): Small parachute or sheet — bounce a ball on it.

VariationBall roll around the parachute — requires watching others and timing.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Industry: the child who contributes to a group goal is experiencing the satisfaction of social usefulness.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Narrate the Cooperation

Parents learn to name and celebrate cooperative moments: 'you and Sam worked together — did you see that?'

🏠 Try This at Home

Cooperative challenge at home — 'let's move all the cushions from the lounge to the bedroom together.' Celebrate the completion.

Equipment
Small parachute or large sheetSoft ball

Self-Help Pod
Contextual Dressing — Hat and Jacket On/Off
ADL: Dressing
▾
Why This Matters

Contextual dressing introduces the idea that dressing decisions are linked to environment — building cognitive flexibility alongside the physical skill.

Activities
  • Remove hat indoors (EYLF 3): Consistent prompt every time entering indoors.
  • Put on hat outdoors (EYLF 3): Child manages this before jackets.
  • Jacket on/off (EYLF 3): Jacket on floor, arms in, stand up — the 'floor flip' technique.

VariationTeach the jacket floor flip: lay jacket on floor, child puts arms in, flips it over their head.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Parents carry children's hats and jackets rather than giving the child the opportunity.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Rule-based dressing — 'hat outside, no hat inside' — builds both self-help skill and early rule-understanding.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Support Then Release
🏠 Try This at Home

Every time you go outside, wait for your child to put their own hat on. Every time you come inside, wait for them to take it off.

Equipment
Child's own hat and jacketLow hook at child height
