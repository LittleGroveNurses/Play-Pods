Week 12

Week 12 is consolidation week. The focus is celebrating how far children and parents have come, and setting families up for continuing practice at home. The final session is also about community — naming peers, celebrating shared experience, and acknowledging the parents' own learning.

Gross Motor Pod
Fine Motor: Drawing Lines and Circles
FMS: Controlled Mark-Making
▾
Why This Matters

Horizontal and vertical lines followed by circular marks are the pre-writing shapes that underpin all letter formation. Week 12 consolidates fine motor mark-making and introduces formal pre-writing context.

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Hold with fingers not fist
Horizontal line — pull across the page
Vertical line — push down the page
Circular scribble — keep going around
Look at the line you're making
Activities
  • Draw simple lines (EYLF 4): Horizontal, then vertical, then diagonal.
  • Trace in sand (EYLF 4): Draw lines in a sand tray — builds motor memory.
  • Paint brushes (EYLF 4): Large brushes on big paper — deliberate strokes.

VariationTrace over dotted lines on paper — visual guidance for direction and length.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Draw lines together on big paper. Ask your child to draw you a line — horizontal, vertical, circular. Praise the control, not the product.

🫀 Body Awareness This Week

Before snack: "Check in with your body — hungry, a little hungry, or not hungry yet?" Let their answer lead the snack. This is 12 weeks of interoceptive practice in one moment — their body, their signal, their choice.

Communication Pod
50+ Word Vocabulary — Consolidation and Celebration
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

By 24 months, children with typical development are expected to have 50+ words. Many entering Play Pods will not be at this milestone. This final week celebrates progress without comparing to an external benchmark — the measure is growth from the child's own starting point.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — this final week, reflect on your own OWL practice. Which was easiest — Observe, Wait, or Listen? Which did you find hardest? Share with the group.

Activities
  • 50 word review (EYLF 5): Lay out picture cards from all 12 weeks. How many can this child now name?
  • Scavenger hunt (EYLF 2): How many things can we name in 5 minutes?
  • Pretend shop (EYLF 5): 'What would you like? How many? Here you go.' Vocabulary in social context.

VariationMake a 'word book' for each child — photographs of things they can now name.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate Growth

Parents identify and name the communication growth they have witnessed over 12 weeks.

🏠 Try This at Home

Make a word book at home — photographs of 10 things your child can name. Look at it together each day. Add to it every week for the rest of the year.

The Gruffalo — Julia Donaldson

Rich vocabulary, memorable characters, cumulative narrative — a celebration of 12 weeks of language building. Read it together and notice how many words your child now knows. Let them tell you what comes next.

Cognitive Pod
Routine Awareness — Consolidation
EF: WM (multi-step routine)
▾
Why This Matters

A child who can complete a familiar routine without prompting — pack up, wash hands, come to the table — is demonstrating working memory, inhibitory control, and cognitive flexibility in an integrated, real-world context. This is the summative EF achievement of the program.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Working Memory (multi-step routine) + Inhibitory Control (complete each step before moving on)

Activities
  • Pack up toys (EYLF 3): Full group pack-away without individual prompts.
  • Wash hands (EYLF 3): Independent completion of the full handwashing sequence.
  • Snack song routine (EYLF 5): Complete the transition using only the song as a cue.

VariationTime the routine — builds self-monitoring.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate Routine Mastery

Parents recognise how much EF their child is demonstrating in familiar routines — and commit to maintaining those routines at home.

🏠 Try This at Home

Run one familiar routine completely without verbal prompts — just the song or signal. Notice how much your child can do independently now.

Social-Emotional Pod
Recognise Peers — Names, Photos, Farewells
CASEL: Social Awareness
▾
Why This Matters

A well-managed ending teaches children that goodbyes are safe. Naming the people in this group and marking the ending with a ritual consolidates the social identity work of the whole program.

Activities
  • Name friends (EYLF 1): 'Who's here today?' Name each child. Can anyone name all the friends?
  • Photo peers (EYLF 1): Photos of the group — 'who is this? do you know their name?'
  • Hello/goodbye ritual (EYLF 1): Consistent group goodbye — song, wave, or handshake.

VariationMake a group photo card for each child to take home.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Social identity: the child who can name their peers and participate in a shared farewell understands themselves as a member of a community.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate the Parent's Journey

The facilitator acknowledges what parents have learned and practised over 12 weeks. The final parent coaching moment: 'you are now your child's best developmental partner.'

🏠 Try This at Home

Talk about the friends from Play Pods at home. Look at the group photo, name them. Community doesn't end when the program does.

Equipment
Group photo cards for each childFarewell songCertificate or memento for each family

Self-Help Pod
Carrying and Contributing — Consolidation
ADL: IADL + All ADL categories reviewed
▾
Why This Matters

The final self-help session consolidates the IADL theme — contributing to the group. The child who can carry, deliver, and help with pack-away at Week 12 is demonstrating the integrated self-help independence that is the goal of the program.

Activities
  • Deliver cup (EYLF 3): Child delivers cups to each peer at snack.
  • Carry snack plate (EYLF 3): Child carries their own snack from the preparation area.
  • Help with pack-away (EYLF 3): Full participation in group pack-away.

VariationGive each child a 'graduation job' — a special helper role for the final session.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Parents may want to do everything for the child on the final session to make it special. The most special thing you can do is step back and watch what they can do now.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Industry consolidated: the child who completes Week 12 has been building the experience of 'I am capable' across 12 weeks.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Celebrate and Release

Parents witness and name what their child can now do independently that they couldn't do 12 weeks ago.

🏠 Try This at Home

Give your child a regular household helper role this week — and keep it. The program ends, the routine continues.
