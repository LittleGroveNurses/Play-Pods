Week 7

Week 7 introduces action word naming in communication, fine motor scribbling in the motor pod, pretend play with roles in cognitive, comfort-seeking and calm corner in social-emotional, and routine helper roles in self-help.

Gross Motor Pod
Fine Motor: Scribbling
FMS: Grip and Mark-Making
▾
Why This Matters

Fine motor development at 2 years focuses on grip transition and mark-making intentionality. Vertical and horizontal lines precede circular scribbles. This week gives the gross motor pod a fine motor focus.

🏃 FMS Technique Cues (Munch & Move)
Hold the crayon with fingers not fist
Whole arm movement for big marks
Wrist rotation for circular scribbles
Press with intention
Change direction intentionally
Activities
  • Crayons (EYLF 4): Large crayons, unlined paper. Encourage vertical strokes first, then horizontal, then circles.
  • Sidewalk chalk (EYLF 4): Outdoor mark-making with whole body engagement.
  • Chunky markers (EYLF 4): Lower resistance than crayons, good for hand strength.

VariationPaint with water on concrete — disappears quickly, encouraging repetition.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Scaffold and Step Back
🏠 Try This at Home

Leave paper and crayons accessible on a low table. Let your child scribble whenever they choose. Ask 'tell me about this' rather than 'what is it?'

🗣️
Communication Pod
Naming Actions
🦉 OWL
▾
Why This Matters

Action words (verbs) are the engine of sentence building. Children with a strong verb vocabulary move faster into multi-word combinations than those with strong noun vocabulary alone.

🦉 OWL Reminder — Observe, Wait, Listen

OWL — Watch: before labelling what your child is doing, watch them do it for 3 seconds. Then name the action as they do it — not before.

Activities
  • Lotto action cards (EYLF 5): Picture cards showing actions. Match card to action.
  • Act 'jump/run' (EYLF 3): Adult says action word — child performs it. Then swap.
  • Pretend doll play actions (EYLF 5): 'Baby is sleeping. Baby is eating. Baby is crying.'

VariationUse photographs of family members doing actions.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Describe and Narrate
🏠 Try This at Home

Narrate what you're doing throughout the day: 'I'm cutting, stirring, pouring, driving.' Every verb you say is a verb they're absorbing.

Equipment
Action lotto cardsDoll for pretend play

Cognitive Pod
Pretend Play — Roles and Sequences
EF: IC (stay in role)
▾
Why This Matters

Harvard (2014): during intentional imaginary play, children develop rules to guide their actions in playing roles. They hold complex ideas in mind and inhibit impulses that don't fit the role — among the strongest EF-building activities at this developmental stage.

🧠 Executive Function Skill This Week

Inhibitory Control (stay in role) + Working Memory (hold the role's rules in mind)

Activities
  • Feed doll (EYLF 1): Spoon, bowl, and doll. Child feeds doll, narrates actions.
  • Pretend sleep (EYLF 1): Blanket the doll, say 'shhh, baby sleeping,' tiptoe.
  • Pretend phone (EYLF 5): Hold toy phone, say 'hello' — wait for child to respond.

VariationIntroduce a doctor's kit — adds new role vocabulary.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Follow Child's Lead
🏠 Try This at Home

Leave a doll or teddy with small props (spoon, blanket, cup) accessible. Don't direct — observe what your child does.

Equipment
Doll with propsToy phoneDoctor's kit (variation)

Social-Emotional Pod
Comfort-Seeking and Self-Regulation
CASEL: Self-Management
▾
Why This Matters

Self-regulation at 2 years is primarily co-regulation. The calm corner is a long-term investment — it becomes more powerful the more familiar it is.

Activities
  • Hug soft toy (EYLF 1): Model self-soothing with a soft toy. 'When I feel upset I hug teddy.'
  • Offer blanket (EYLF 3): Soft blanket in the calm area. Weighted or textured objects add proprioceptive regulation.
  • Calm corner (EYLF 3): Designate a quiet corner. Introduce it proactively — not only when a child is dysregulated.

VariationAdd a simple feelings chart to the calm corner.

🎯 Facilitator Coaching Cues

When a parent minimises distress: "He's having a big feeling. Try sitting close, saying 'I can see you're upset' — and just wait with him. You don't need to fix it, just be present."

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Autonomy through security: the child who trusts that their distress will be witnessed without dismissal is building the emotional security that makes later independence possible.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Co-Regulate First

Parents learn that co-regulation — being a calm presence alongside the dysregulated child — is the only pathway to eventual self-regulation.

🏠 Try This at Home

Create a small calm corner at home — a cushion, soft toy, and one or two books in a quiet spot. Show your child where it is when they're calm.

Equipment
Soft toys and blanketsCalm corner setupFeelings chart

Self-Help Pod
Routine Helper Roles
ADL: IADL
▾
Why This Matters

Giving 2-year-olds real roles in household routines builds agency, sequencing, and a sense of contribution.

Activities
  • Carry bib to table (EYLF 3): Child carries their own bib from the hook to their highchair.
  • Put toy in box (EYLF 3): Single-item pack-away. Name the toy as it goes in.
  • Join pack-away song (EYLF 3): Cleanup song that begins the transition.

VariationGive the child a 'helper badge' — even a sticker makes the role feel real.

⚠️ Common Parent Trap

Parents often do the routine task for the child to save time, then ask the child to do something 'helpful' that isn't real. Even one real task builds more agency than ten pretend tasks.

🌱 Development Note (Erikson)

Industry begins here: the child given a real role in household routines experiences themselves as capable and contributing.

👪 Parent Coaching Focus: Support Then Release
🏠 Try This at Home

Give your child one real job at dinner this week — same job every night so it becomes their role.

Equipment
Helper badge (sticker)Pack-away song
